SLS Hopper


For my capstone project, I worked closely with 3 other engineering seniors to redesign a powder dispensing system for a selective laser sintering (SLS) 3D printer. We were working on an experimental system developed at UT for research on new materials and fabrication processes.

The original hopper system, seen below, had several flaws. Since the powder was extremely fine, it was difficult to contain. The notched roller within the hopper was intended to dispense equal amounts to powder every cycle, similar to a toothpick dispenser, but the mechanism was not reliable and often clogged along its grooves.

After weeks of concept generation, we came up with 3 prototypes, shown below. The first was our control, matching the original hopper design. The second was a modification of this design, where the roller picked up powder from the side, reducing the packing force of the powder in the hopper to mitigate clumping. Our third design attempted to negate the effect of gravity on powder leakage, as well as introduce planar rather than cylindrical sealing. This simple mechanism had a sliding plate that filled with powder and dropped it when linearly displaced.

Our experiments highlighted some of the benefits of the sliding mechanism, so we decided to pursue this design. Since this was a new concept, we decided to build a desktop model rather than implementing it on the actual machine. After some engineering analysis, we started our design and decided to actuate the sliding plate with a rack and pinion on a simple PD controller.
